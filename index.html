ECDIS (ELECTRONIC CHART DISPLAY INFORMATION SYSTEM)

Defination

An Electronic Chart Display and Information System (ECDIS) is a geographic information system used for nautical navigation that complies with International Maritime Organization (IMO) regulations as an alternative to paper nautical charts.

Uses of ECDIS

ECDIS provides continuous position and navigational safety information. The system generates audible and/or visual alarms when the vessel is in proximity to navigational hazards. Military versions of ECDIS are known as WECDIS (warship ECDIS) or ECDIS-N (ECDIS-naval).

The amended SOLAS regulation V/19 requires all newly built passenger ships of 500 gross tonnage and upwards, as well as newly built cargo ships of 3,000 gross tonnage and upwards engaged on international voyages to be fitted with ECDIS.

ENC (ELECTRONIC NAVIGATIONAL CHARTS)

An electronic navigational chart or ENC is an official database created by a national hydrographic office for use with an Electronic Chart Display and Information System (ECDIS).

India also have national hydrographic office in Dehradun, Uttarakhand

Electronic Navigational Charts (ENC), also known as vector charts, are data sets to support all types of nautical navigation

Errors of ECDIS

Source Error - An electronic chart can be as good as the original data it was derived from. Position shift : There will be position shift shown between GPS and actual position on the chart if the GPS and the chart are using different datum's.

Limitations of ECDIS

1. Lack of Global coverage

The lack of official ENC makes things just worse. Even if a number of Hydrographic Offices have started ambitious programs of ENC production, very few official electronic charts in S-57/3 format are available today. The result is that ECDIS users have to supplement ENC data with non-ENC electronic charts.
